Marie Curie Timeline

1867

Marie Curie is born in Warsaw, Poland.

1891

She moves to Paris to study science and mathematics.

1898

Marie Curie discovers two new elements: polonium and radium.

1903

She wins the Nobel Prize in Physics.

1934

Marie Curie passes away at the age of 66.

Marie Curie was a Polish-born scientist born in 1867. She discovered two new elements, polonium and radium, which changed the study of science and medicine. In her research, Marie pioneered the study of radioactivity, which is why she is considered one of the most important scientists in history. She believed in the power of science to help people, and she is an important role model for girls in STEAM.
